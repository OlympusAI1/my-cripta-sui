A letter without expectation

I've realized that, deep down, this desire has always been there, from the very beginning. And what is it? Is it a search? Is it always asking why? Asking the teacher, the mother, the relatives → why this? why that? And almost never getting an answer.

But it's not just that. It's that, when you do find the answer to that why, something inside you becomes so clear, you can't keep it to yourself. Because that answer isn't just valid for you. It holds meaning for others too. Or even if it's not the same answer for someone else, what matters is to share the search itself, to communicate the search, to share the journey.

There is no stage that simply ends there, once and for all. Every step, every experience, carries something that is still alive.

To communicate is to share the experience. To share what makes sense to you, but it's a kind of meaning that belongs to everyone. It's something that cannot be hidden in a treasure chest. What brings life to me, brings life to others as well. Why should it belong only to me? Why should it matter only to me?

From this, over time, came the meaning of this blog. In writing, there is a kind of offering. You offer what is essential in your soul, in your choices.

And this offering isn't about expecting the reader to understand, or to respond. It is simply an act of offering.
